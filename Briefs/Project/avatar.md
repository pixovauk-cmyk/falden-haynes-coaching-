## How to use this document

Before writing any piece of content, ask: which avatar is this for?
Every page, every ad, every LinkedIn post should speak to ONE avatar — not all three at once.
The more specifically you write for one person, the more everyone in that group feels seen.

## Avatar 1 — Jordan (Primary)
### "The Aspiring Founder"
**Age:** 20–27 · **Location:** West Midlands / UK · **Channel:** Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok

### Who Jordan is
Jordan has a business idea. Has had it for months — maybe longer. Talks about it to friends. Has probably started a Instagram page or registered a name. But hasn't actually launched. Every week there's a new reason: not ready, not enough money, not sure where to start.

Jordan is motivated. Genuinely. But motivation without structure just becomes anxiety. Jordan doesn't need more inspiration — Jordan needs a plan.

Works a job they tolerate. Scrolls LinkedIn at lunch. Watches entrepreneur content on YouTube. Has downloaded free business plan templates and never finished them. Thinks maybe they need a mentor but doesn't know where to find one who isn't either too expensive or too generic.

### What Jordan is feeling
- "I know I want to do this but I don't know where to start"
- "What if I do all this work and it fails?"
- "I don't want to waste money on a coach who just tells me things I already know"
- "Everyone else seems further ahead than me"
- "I feel embarrassed that I haven't launched yet"

### What Jordan actually needs
Not another YouTube video. Not another free template. A real conversation with someone who has actually built businesses — and a written plan they can execute the week after the call.

### Price sensitivity
High. £35–50 for a first session feels achievable. £100+ feels like a risk without knowing if it's worth it. The launch pricing is specifically designed for Jordan — it removes the financial excuse.

### What makes Jordan trust Feldon
- He's built real businesses (35+) — not just read about them
- He's relatable — not corporate, not 50 years old in a suit
- Real testimonials from people who look like Jordan
- Authenticity over polish — a video of Feldon talking naturally does more than a professional ad

### Bundle fit
**Business Plan Bundle** — 6 sessions, £160. Takes Jordan from idea to a complete, executable plan.

### How to write for Jordan
- Short sentences. Energy. Direct.
- Acknowledge the feeling of being stuck before talking about the solution
- Use "you" constantly — make it feel personal
- Never talk down. Jordan is smart — just needs direction.
- Show the outcome: "You'll leave every session with a written action plan"
- Don't use corporate words. Jordan doesn't speak "leverage your core competencies"

### Example headline that works for Jordan
*"You've got the idea. You just need the plan. Let's build it together."*

## Avatar 2 — Marcus (Secondary)
### "The Early-Stage Founder"
**Age:** 25–38 · **Location:** West Midlands / UK · **Channel:** LinkedIn, email, referral

### Who Marcus is
Marcus is already running a business — under 2 years in. Making some money. Has customers. But something feels off. Growing in the wrong direction, or not growing at all. Spending time on everything and making progress on nothing. 

Knows the basics. Has a Companies House registration, maybe a basic website. But the business is starting to feel chaotic instead of exciting. Marcus is doing things reactively rather than strategically.

Has probably tried to do it alone — watched Gary Vee, read a few business books, maybe attended a free Growth Hub session that was too generic to be useful. Knows what a business plan is but hasn't updated theirs since they started.

### What Marcus is feeling
- "I'm busy but I don't feel like I'm building anything"
- "I don't know which of my services to focus on"
- "I need someone to help me see this from the outside"
- "I'm not sure if I'm growing or just spinning"
- "The free advice I've had has been too generic — I need someone to understand MY business"

### What Marcus actually needs
Strategic clarity. An outside perspective from someone who has actually operated businesses — not just advised on them. A session that ends with a clear 90-day plan, not more questions.

### Price sensitivity
Medium. £50–75 per session feels reasonable if the outcome is clear. More willing to invest in a bundle if he can see what he gets from each session. Values ROI over cost.

### What makes Marcus trust Feldon
- Track record with real businesses across sectors
- The awards — particularly the EPIC Spirit Award signals real-world perseverance, not just theory
- Testimonials from founders at a similar stage
- Feldon's willingness to be direct and give honest opinions, not just ask questions

### Bundle fit
**Direction Bundle** (6 sessions, £160) for strategic clarity, or **Action Sprint Bundle** (3 sessions, £90) if he has a specific blocker to break through.

### How to write for Marcus
- Confident, peer-level tone — not coach talking down to student
- Lead with the problem: chaos, lost direction, spinning wheels
- Show that Feldon understands business operations, not just startup theory
- Use outcome language: "After session 1 you'll have a clear picture of where your business actually is"
- Slightly more professional than Jordan — Marcus reads LinkedIn properly

### Example headline that works for Marcus
*"You're running the business. But is the business running you?"*

## Avatar 3 — Diane (Tertiary)
### "The Wider Sector Client"
**Age:** 30–55 · **Sector:** Charity / social enterprise / private business · **Channel:** Events, referral, LinkedIn

### Who Diane is
Diane runs an established organisation — or leads a significant function within one. Could be a charity director, a social enterprise founder, or a business owner with a team. Has a specific challenge: a new project, a strategic pivot, a compliance gap, a team issue.

Comes to Feldon through a referral or after hearing him speak at an event. Already has a level of credibility herself — so she needs Feldon to match that energy, not talk to her like she's a beginner.

Values professionalism and discretion. Expects preparation. Wants someone who will actually engage with the specifics of her situation, not give her generic frameworks.

### What Diane is feeling
- "I need an objective perspective on this — someone outside the organisation"
- "I don't want to go to a big consultancy and spend £5,000 for a PDF"
- "I've heard good things about Feldon — I want to see if he gets our sector"
- "I need this done properly, not quickly"

### What Diane actually needs
Focused, structured thinking applied to a real organisational challenge. A session plan that respects her time. Written outputs she can take back to her board or team. Someone who has operated across sectors and understands the nuance.

### Price sensitivity
Low — relative to other avatars. Budget exists if the value is clear. More likely to buy a Project or Direction Bundle outright than start with a single session.

### What makes Diane trust Feldon
- The awards, particularly the industry credibility they signal
- Evidence of working across public, private, and third sector
- Clear, professional communication
- Referral from someone she already trusts
- The structured approach — written action summaries, session plans

### Bundle fit
**Project Bundle** (3 sessions, £90) for a specific project, or **Direction Bundle** (6 sessions, £160) for wider strategic work.

### How to write for Diane
- More formal than Jordan and Marcus — but not stiff
- Lead with credibility and track record
- Emphasise the written outputs and structured approach
- Show sector breadth — not just startup experience
- She reads the full page, not just the headline — give her substance

### Example headline that works for Diane
*"Strategic support for growing organisations. Focused, structured, and grounded in real experience."*
